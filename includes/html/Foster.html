Foster



Why should you foster a dog?
  1. Fostering a dog is one of the most rewarding experiences you can have.
  2. Freeing up a spot so the shelter or rescue can take in another dog.
  3. Giving your foster dog the time he needs to be ready for adoption.
  4. Helping the shelter or rescue learn more about the dog so he can end up in the best home possible.
  5. Socializing the dog to a home environment and possibly getting him used to being around other pets and different types of people.


What is the role of the foster family?

The goal of the foster family is to accompany the dog in training in its first year, to turn it into a mature, educated, trained puppy.

The foster family must assist in the entire process of raising the puppy and its exposure to as many types of people, populations, noises, residential areas, different contact, etc.

The puppy comes to the foster family at a young age (about 3 months), The puppies are not yet used to needs and at such an age they still use their mouth and teeth a lot.

The center accompanies the foster families closely throughout their journey.

The foster families meet once every two weeks accompanied by a professional staff member who coordinates the center for training and a meeting between all the families and the puppies in training.
